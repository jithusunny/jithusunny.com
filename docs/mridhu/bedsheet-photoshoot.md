BEDSHEET CONTENT SHOOT — QUICK PLAYBOOK

Purpose: capture clean, natural visuals for first sale.
Follow once. No perfection.

BEFORE YOU START (5 minutes)
- Clean phone camera lens
- Choose window with soft daylight
- Turn OFF all indoor lights
- Clear background (no clutter)
- Iron bedsheet lightly (don’t overdo)

SETUP
- Place bedsheet on bed or floor near window
- Let fabric fall naturally (don’t stretch tight)
- Stand sideways to the window (light from the side)
- Hold phone straight (not tilted)
- Use Photo mode (not Portrait)

PHOTO SHOTS (DO IN THIS ORDER)

SHOT 1 — HERO (FULL BED)
- Bedsheet fully spread
- Corners visible
- Step back slightly
- Take 4 photos
- Slightly adjust angle between shots

SHOT 2 — ANGLED FULL VIEW
- Stand at bed corner
- Shoot diagonally across bed
- Take 3 photos

SHOT 3 — FOLDED NEAT
- Fold into clean rectangle
- Place near window
- Shoot from eye level
- Take 3 photos

SHOT 4 — FOLDED CLOSE-UP
- Move closer
- Focus on layers and folds
- Side light visible
- Take 2 photos

SHOT 5 — TEXTURE CLOSE-UP
- Zoom slightly
- Fabric weave visible
- Light from side
- Take 3 photos

SHOT 6 — EDGE / CORNER DETAIL
- Show stitching or border
- Shoot at slight angle
- Take 2 photos

SHOT 7 — HUMAN TOUCH
- Hand smoothing fabric or holding fold
- No face
- Natural movement
- Take 3 photos

VIDEO SHOTS (IMPORTANT)

VIDEO 1 — SLOW PAN
- Start at one corner
- Slowly move phone across bed
- Keep movement steady
- 6–8 seconds
- Shoot twice

VIDEO 2 — FABRIC FLOW
- Gently lift one corner
- Let it fall naturally
- Slow motion if available
- 2 takes

VIDEO 3 — FOLD / UNFOLD
- Start folded
- Unfold slowly
- Keep frame stable
- 2 takes

QUICK CHECK BEFORE STOPPING
- At least 8–10 good photos
- At least 2 usable videos
- No yellow light
- No heavy filters

AFTER SHOOT (OPTIONAL)
- Brightness +5 to +10
- Contrast +5
- Reduce warmth if yellow
- Do NOT over-edit
